Program Overview

The 4+1 Dual Degree Engineering Program is a partnership between Saint Mary’s College and the University of Notre Dame that enables students to earn two degrees in just five years: a Bachelor of Science in Computing and Applied Mathematics (CAM) and a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science. This program combines the strengths of a liberal arts education with top-tier engineering training.

Why Choose the 4+1 Dual Degree Program?

  • Two Degrees, One Cohesive Path: Students graduate with both a CAM degree from Saint Mary’s and a CS degree from Notre Dame, opening doors in both industry and academia.
  • Accelerated Timeline: Complete both degrees in five years instead of the traditional six or more, saving time and tuition costs.
  • Interdisciplinary Foundation: The CAM program strengthens mathematical reasoning and analytical thinking while CS training at Notre Dame builds strong technical skills.
  • Advising from Two Institutions: Dual advising teams help students navigate course selection, research opportunities, internships, and the transition between campuses.
  • Expanded Career Opportunities: Graduates are uniquely positioned for careers in software engineering, data science, tech consulting, and graduate studies in engineering or applied mathematics.
  • Access to Resources: Take advantage of student organizations, career centers, research labs, and internship networks at both Saint Mary’s and Notre Dame.
  • Small Class Sizes + Big Campus Access: Benefit from personalized attention at Saint Mary’s while gaining experience with Notre Dame’s new technology and infrastructure.
