Saint Mary's students are expected to be able to proficiently communicate within their discipline. As a part of your education as a student, you will go through the W program. The W program consists of two stages, with the first beginning when you first arrive and the second following you throughout your career. The first step is the Basic W and the second is the Advanced W.

Basic W

This can be satisfid by general education courses, it essentially tests your foundation in your writing skills. For the Basic W you'll need to be able create a logical thesis, organize your supporting evidence, and effectively communicate your ideas

Suggested Courses

There are a variety general of eductaion courses that can fulfill the "Basic W", but usually a 100 level literature class is taken to fulfill this requirement. However, the Basic W can be fulfilled in COMM 103W (Introduction to Communication Studies W), HIST 201W (U.S. History to 1865 W, ICS 201W), and SOC 203W (Social Problems). Of course there a variety of other courses that can be taken to fulfill the Basic W as well as fulfill general education requirements.

Specific Requirements

  • At least one in class essay
  • At least two thesis-driven analytical essays
  • Optional: one creative essay

The readers will read your top three essays, and will usually read them in the order that they were submitted in. If they want to see more of your writing they will review the rest of the essays within your folder.

Advanced W

Purpose

The 'Advanced W' help build upon your basic writing skills and apply it to your major. The skills include understanding relevant theories and terms, using the knowledge accumalated in the CAM major to make logical arguments,and finding reliable information.

Types of Papers

As a requirement for graduating, each student must successfully complete three of the four types of the following papers:

  • Literature Review

    An in-depth summary and critical analysis of a topic within the field of interest. It brings together existing research, key themes, and controversies for the area. A literature review is more objective than a position paper while also demonstrating the writer's points of disagreement. The author writes for an audience of educated peers.

  • Research Report

    This report explains a question of interest, reviews relevant literature, states a hypothesis, defines variables, describes the research design, and includes data and analyses if available. It should include an abstract, method, results, and references, for an audience of educated peers.

  • Position Paper

    This paper presents a clear stance on an issue within the field, using strong evidence, examples, and an understanding of opposing views while writing for an audience of educated peers.

  • Reflection Paper

    This paper is the most personalized to the author. It allows them to use psychological constructs as tools for self-discovery. Reviewers look for writing that is thoughtful, original, theoretical, and uses examples.

  • Case Study/Lab Report

    A paper that reports and examines an individual’s findings over time.
