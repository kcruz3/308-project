Saint Mary's students are expected to be able to proficiently communicate within their discipline. As a part of your education as a student, you will go through the W program. The W program consists of two stages, with the first beginning when you first arrive and the second following you throughout your career. The first stae is the Basic W and the second is the Advanced W.

Basic W

This can be satisfid by general education courses, it essentially testing your foundation in your writing. For this you'll need to be able create a logical thesis, organize and effectively communicate your ideas

Suggested Courses

There are a variety general of eductaion courses that can fulfill the "Basic W", but usually a 100 level literature class is taken to fulfill this requirement.

Specific Requirements

  • At least one in class essay
  • At least two thesis-driven analytical essays
  • Optional: one creative essay

The readers will read your top thyree essays, and will usually read them in the order that they were submitted in. If they want to see more of your writing they will review the rest of the essays within your folder.

Advanced W

Purpose

The 'Advanced W' help build upon your basic writing skills and apply it to your major. The skills include understanding relevant theories and terms, using the knowledge accumalated in the CAM major to make logical arguments,and finding reliable information.

Types of Papers

As a requirement for graduating, each student must successfully complete three of the four types of the following papers:

  • Literature Review

    • An in depth summary and critical analysis of a topic within the field of interest. It brings together existing research, key themes, and controversies for the area. A literature review is more objective than a position paper, while also demonstrating the writers points of disagreements. The auuthor writes for an audience of educated peers.

  • Research Report

    • This writing report explains a question of interest, reviews relevant literature, states a hypothesis, defines variables, describes the research design, and includes data and anlyses if available. It should be with an abstract, method, results, and references, for an audience of educated peers.

  • Position Paper

    • This type of paper presents a clear stance on a issue within the field, using strong evidence, examples, and an understanding of opposing views while writing for an audience of educated peers.

  • Reflection Paper

    • This paper is the most personalized to the author, it allows them to use psycological constructs as tools for self-discovery. Reviewers of this look for writing which is thoughtful, original, using theories, and uses examples.

  • Case Study/Lab Report

    • A paper that reports and examines an individuals findings over time.

Common Reasons for Revision

  • Failure to Revise
  • Unclear Ideas
  • Poor Communication
    • Unclear Referents
    • Overuse of vague words
    • Not clarifying statements

      • For example clarifying when using the word significant when referring to a study, like if that was meant statistically, reliable, clinically important, or notable?

Plagiarism

Plagiarism is defined as using someones work and display it as your own. Without giving credit, plagiarism includes not only using eaxct wording without quotation marks but also copying the patterns and structure of someoine else's ideas.
